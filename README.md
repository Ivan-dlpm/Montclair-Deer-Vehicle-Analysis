# 🦌 Where Deer Meet Roads: A Five-Year Spatial & Temporal Analysis of Deer-Vehicle Incidents in Montclair, NJ

**Township of Montclair – Department of Sustainability | Data Analytics Internship**
**January – May 2026**

---

## Project Overview

This project presents the first systematic, GIS-based analysis of deer-vehicle incidents in Montclair, New Jersey, conducted during a data analytics internship with the Township of Montclair's Department of Sustainability. Using five years of verified municipal invoice data, the study identifies where, when, and why deer-vehicle incidents are occurring — and what the township can do about it.

Between 2020 and 2025, Montclair recorded **222 confirmed deer carcass removal events**, representing a **117% increase** over the study period. The findings reveal clear spatial hotspots, predictable seasonal peaks, and an evidence-based policy framework for targeted intervention.

## Tools & Methods

- **Data Collection:** Municipal deer carcass removal invoices (Township of Montclair Health Dept., 2020–2025)
- **Data Cleaning & Preparation:** Manual extraction, address standardization, and structured compilation in Excel
- **GIS & Spatial Analysis:** ArcGIS Pro — geocoding, point mapping, kernel density estimation (KDE)
- **Cartographic Design:** National Geographic basemap + World Hillshade layer for topographic context
- **Statistical Analysis:** Year-over-year trends, monthly distributions, seasonal breakdowns, and street-level hotspot rankings

## Policy Recommendations

The spatial and temporal structure of Montclair's deer incidents supports a **layered, evidence-based intervention strategy**:

1. **Upper Mountain Ave** — Priority corridor for infrastructure assessment (exclusion fencing, guided crossing points, reflective barriers)
2. **Seasonal signage** — Deploy dynamic deer advisory messaging on changeable message signs along western corridors each September–November
3. **Hotspot-targeted warnings** — Rotating seasonal warning signs at the top 5 incident streets during peak rut window
4. **Avoid ineffective measures** — Standard static deer signs, deer whistles, and passive roadside reflectors have little to no documented effect on collision rates
